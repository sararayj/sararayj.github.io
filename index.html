1. Introduction¶

According to UNICEF, Child marriage refers to any formal marriage or informal union between a child under the age of 18 and an adult or another child(https://www.unicef.org/protection/child-marriage). Child marriage is one of the world problems such as poverty and environmental pollution. Unfortunately, many people do not know about child marriage problem and why it matters. Families with financial difficulties often marry young daughters to receive a dowry from a married man to the brides family. They usually get married early because of their parents pressure. Forced child marriage is obviously sexual and emotional abuse of a girl. Children who are not yet fully grown adults are deprived of education and work opportunities by early marriage. Since adolescence is not physically and mentally mature, if they become pregnant at that time, they may die from childbirth complications. If you want to know more problems about child marriage, visit this site. https://www.savethechildren.org/us/charity-stories/child-marriage-a-violation-of-child-rights

Even now, many girls still suffer as victims of early marriage. It is not a problem that can be solved immediately, but knowing that this is a problem can be the starting point for you to join in solving it. Among the countries around the world, I will find out which countries have high child marriage rates and what are the common characteristics of those countries. Based on this, I will find out what solutions can be proposed to lower the child marriage rate through various data. Specifically, I will look at the average marriage age data for each country to find out which countries marry at an early age. In addition, I will look at the relationship between the countrys GDP, education level, and marriage age.

The World Health Organization provides intimate partner violence rate data for each country. Unfortunately, the page was dynamic, so it was not easy to crawl data. So I collected the data one by one and created them into a csv file and attached it. You can visit the original source website click here: https://srhr.org/vaw-data/data
